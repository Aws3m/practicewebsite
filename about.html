My journey

IT friends

My friends are in IT and let me see the apps they were building and test some of them. The more i did, the more I got intrigued and wanted to learn how to do it myself

College

I started a bachelors in IT. This was hard with my busy family life back then. So i decided i couldn't complete it at the time. I searched for a more specific education and found one.

Started evening school to graduate as a front-end developer

I started going to evening school at Syntra. A Belgian school for adults to learn to become a front-end developer. Here we learned to make full web applications

Started as Full-stack developer

In my second year of Syntra I started working as an intern for a branding company as a full-stack developer. Though I leaned a lot here I didn't stay here.

Start as a self employed web developer/designer

I realised that I wanted to work for myself and have direct contact with clients to match their needs.

My work ethics

Responsive

Your website should respond to any screen size it's displayed on, whether it will be a laptop, tablet or phone, your website should look good. I make it happen, that's a promise!

Mobile First

80% of your website visits are phone users. So it comes naturally that your website is developed with that in mind. So we make it look good on your phone first, and move on from there to a tablet and laptop screen.

Cyber Security

The world wide web has been plagued with viruses and malware. Your website will be secure and the information you recieve from email will be subject to extreme caution to GDPR rules and safety measurements
